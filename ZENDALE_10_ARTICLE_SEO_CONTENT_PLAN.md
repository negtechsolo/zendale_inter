## On-page SEO standards applied

Each article includes:

- One specific primary keyword and closely related language used naturally.
- A unique SEO title and meta description.
- A clear H1 followed by descriptive H2 sections.
- A concise opening that answers the reader's central question.
- Internal links and a relevant Zendale call to action.
- Full article content rather than a teaser page.
- Frequently asked questions and FAQ structured data.
- Article structured data, canonical URL and Open Graph article metadata.
- Publication date, reading time, author and source list.
- Evidence from recognised primary sources, including WHO, ILO, Nigerian public authorities and the World Bank where relevant.
- No unsupported success rates, patient volumes or medical outcome claims.

## Editorial safeguards

1. Confirm the final public author name before release.
2. Assign a subject reviewer to every article before the branch is merged.
3. Clinical and occupational-health content must remain educational and must not provide individual medical advice.
4. Legal, privacy, investment and public-private partnership content should receive appropriate professional review.
5. Confirm every source link before publication.
6. Replace the temporary Vercel domain in canonical and sitemap settings when the official Zendale Limited domain is connected.
7. Review page titles and snippets in Google Search Console after indexing; SEO optimisation improves discoverability but does not guarantee rankings.
